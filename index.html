Create and launch a Search campaign

Set up, configure, and launch a keyword-targeted Search campaign in the Purplle partner portal. This guide covers defining campaign details, selecting products, adding keywords, and activating your campaign.

Video walkthrough
Coming soon
1
Go to the Purplle partner portal at prism.purplle.com.
2
Click Purplle Ads.
3
Click Search Campaigns.
4
Click + Create New Campaign.
5
Select a wallet.
6
Choose your Primary wallet or a sub-wallet.
7
Give the campaign a name.
8
Set the daily budget.
9
Choose a pacing option — Accelerated or Partitioned.
10
Set a start date and time.
11
Set an end date and time.
12
Find products to advertise by Product, Name, Category, Price, or Set Price.
13
Select all the products you want to advertise.
14
Click Next.
15
Add your keywords.
16
Click the keyword field to enter a keyword.
17
Click Add keyword row to add another keyword.
18
Add any keywords you don't want to target in the Negative keywords section.
19
Click Save & Launch.
20
Search for the campaign you just created.
21
Click Draft.
22
Click Activate to take the campaign live.
Search and Smart Shopping run on separate inventories. Creating a Search campaign here does not affect your Smart Shopping campaigns — set up and check each type separately.

Analyze Search campaign performance

Track and evaluate the metrics that matter for your Search campaigns — attributed GMV, ROI, and more — and break performance down by product, category, brand, and keyword.

Video walkthrough
Coming soon
Campaign navigation
1
Click Purplle Ads in the side navigation.
2
Select Search Campaigns from the expanded menu.
Metric analysis
3
Click the Analytics icon for the campaign you want to analyze.
4
Select the metrics you want from the dropdown, then click Update filter to refresh the view.
5
Open the date range picker, choose your dates, and click Apply to filter the data.
Performance breakdown
6
Open the Products tab for product-level performance.
7
Open the Categories tab.
8
Open the Brand tab.
9
Open the Search Keyword tab to review keyword-level data.
How outcomes are attributed: metrics like attributed GMV and ROI are based on click-through attribution — a sale is credited to your campaign when a shopper clicks your ad and then purchases.
