Create and launch a Search campaign

Set up, configure, and launch a keyword-targeted Search campaign in the Purplle partner portal. This guide covers defining campaign details, selecting products, adding keywords, and activating your campaign.

Video walkthrough
Coming soon
1
Go to the Purplle partner portal at prism.purplle.com.
2
Click Purplle Ads.
3
Click Search Campaigns.
4
Click + Create New Campaign.
5
Select a wallet.
6
Choose your Primary wallet or a sub-wallet.
7
Give the campaign a name.
8
Set the daily budget.
9
Choose a pacing option — Accelerated or Partitioned.
10
Set a start date and time.
11
Set an end date and time.
12
Find products to advertise by Product, Name, Category, Price, or Set Price.
13
Select all the products you want to advertise.
14
Click Next.
15
Add your keywords.
16
Click the keyword field to enter a keyword.
17
Click Add keyword row to add another keyword.
18
Add any keywords you don't want to target in the Negative keywords section.
19
Click Save & Launch.
20
Search for the campaign you just created.
21
Click Draft.
22
Click Activate to take the campaign live.
Search and Smart Shopping run on separate inventories. Creating a Search campaign here does not affect your Smart Shopping campaigns — set up and check each type separately.

Use Campaign Simulation for Smart Shopping

The Campaign Simulation panel lets you preview performance estimates as you set up a Smart Shopping campaign — so you can see how bids and budget affect projected results before you launch.

Video walkthrough
Coming soon
Campaign navigation
1
Go to Purplle Ads in the sidebar.
2
Select Smart Shopping under the Performance section.
3
Click Create New Campaign.
Campaign setup
4
Open the Campaign Simulation panel and review the performance estimates.
5
Enter the campaign name and daily budget.
6
Click Next to proceed to product selection.
Product selection
7
Filter products by category (for example, Skincare > Moisturizer > Serum & Essence).
8
Click Apply Filters to update the product list.
9
Select the products you want to include in the campaign.
10
Click Next to proceed to bidding.
Bidding and finalization
11
Adjust your category bid amounts and watch the estimates update in the simulation tool.
12
Click Next to move to the review stage.
13
Close the Campaign Simulation panel.
14
Click Save & Launch to finalize and start the campaign.
Simulation estimates are projections. The Campaign Simulation panel helps you compare how different bids and budgets could perform. Treat the figures as directional estimates to guide setup, not guaranteed results.

Raise a credit request with PayU

Top up your wallet through the PayU gateway. This walkthrough covers selecting PayU as your payment method and entering your amount, through to the review stage.

Video walkthrough
Coming soon
1
Click Manage in the top navigation bar.
2
Select Raise Credit Request from the dropdown menu.
3
Select PayU (gateway, auto-confirm ≤ ₹4.5L) from the Payment Method dropdown.
4
Enter a net amount of up to ₹4,50,000 and click Next → Step 2.
5
Click Next → Step 3 to proceed to the review stage.
PayU credit requests up to ₹4,50,000 are auto-confirmed. Amounts within this limit are confirmed automatically through the gateway.

Raise a credit request with a bank transfer

Top up your wallet using NEFT, RTGS, or a bank transfer. This walkthrough covers selecting the wallet and payment method, entering your amount, and submitting your UTR and payment proof.

Video walkthrough
Coming soon
Request initiation
1
Click Manage.
2
Click Raise Credit Request.
Transaction setup
3
Select Primary from the Active Wallets list.
4
Select Primary from the Credit To Wallet dropdown.
5
Select NEFT / RTGS / Bank Transfer as the payment method.
6
Enter your net amount (here, ₹2,50,000).
7
Click Next → Step 2.
Payment proof
8
Enter your UTR number and upload your payment proof.
9
Click Next → Step 3.
10
Click Submit Recharge Request.
Bank transfer credits are reviewed before they're applied. Unlike PayU, a NEFT / RTGS / bank transfer request is confirmed after your UTR and payment proof are verified — it isn't credited instantly.
